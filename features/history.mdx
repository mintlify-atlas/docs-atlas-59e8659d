The History view lets you explore your screen captures over time without needing a search query. Browse by app usage or scrub through a visual timeline of your day.

## View Modes

History offers two complementary ways to explore your captures:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Apps Mode" icon="window-restore">
    Activity analytics with app usage breakdown, charts, and daily digest.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Timeline Mode" icon="clock">
    Visual timeline showing screenshots grouped by hour and day.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Apps Mode

Get insights into your app usage and activity patterns.

### Overview Stats

At the top of Apps mode, you'll see:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Screen Time">
    Total active time (excludes idle periods and deduped screenshots).
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Total Captures">
    Number of screenshots taken in the selected time range.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Unique Apps">
    How many different applications you used.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Top App">
    Your most-used application by time.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### App Timeline

A color-coded timeline shows your app activity throughout the day:

- Each app gets a unique color for easy identification
- Hover over segments to see app name and duration
- Gaps indicate idle periods or paused capture
- Click a segment to jump to that app in Timeline mode

### Activity Charts

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Hourly Activity">
    Bar chart showing captures per hour:
    - Identifies your peak productivity hours
    - Shows activity distribution throughout the day
    - Updates every 30 seconds
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="App Usage Donut">
    Proportional breakdown by application:
    - Shows time percentage per app
    - Hover for exact counts
    - Click to filter Timeline mode by app
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Categories">
    Groups apps by activity type:
    - Work, Social, Entertainment, Development, etc.
    - Configurable in Settings → Focus → Category Rules
    - Helps identify distraction patterns
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

### Daily Digest

If Ollama is available, Apps mode shows an AI-generated daily summary:

- Key highlights from your day
- Major tasks and activities
- Time spent in different contexts
- Generated from screenshot metadata and OCR text

<Note>
Daily digest requires Ollama with the chat model configured (default: `qwen2.5:3b`).
</Note>

## Timeline Mode

Browse screenshots chronologically with hour-by-hour organization.

### Timeline Structure

Screenshots are grouped hierarchically:

```
📅 Day Group (e.g., "Today", "Yesterday", "Mon Mar 1")
  ├─ 🕐 Hour Group (e.g., "9:00 AM")
  │   ├─ Screenshot 1
  │   ├─ Screenshot 2
  │   └─ Screenshot 3
  ├─ 🕑 Hour Group (e.g., "10:00 AM")
  │   └─ Screenshot 4
  └─ ...
```

Each hour shows:
- Number of captures in that hour
- Expandable grid of thumbnails
- App diversity indicator

### Navigation

<Steps>
  <Step title="Select time range">
    Use the preset buttons (Today, Yesterday, 7d, 30d) or pick a custom date with the calendar.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Expand hour groups">
    Click on an hour header to expand/collapse screenshots for that hour.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="View screenshot details">
    Click any thumbnail to open the full screenshot with OCR text and metadata.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Navigate between screenshots">
    Use arrow keys or on-screen controls in detail view to move between captures.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Range Selection

Select multiple screenshots to save as a collection:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Enable range select mode">
    Click the **"Select Range"** button (crosshair icon).
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Choose start and end">
    Click on two screenshots or hour groups to define the range.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Review selection">
    The toolbar shows how many screenshots are selected and the time span.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Save as collection">
    Click **"Save as Collection"** and give it a name.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Info>
Collections are accessible from the Saved view. Perfect for grouping related work sessions or project milestones.
</Info>

## Activity Tracking

History view uses active time blocks to calculate accurate screen time:

### Active Block Detection

Active blocks are periods where:
1. Screenshots are being captured
2. Window changes or activity is detected
3. Not marked as excluded (e.g., password managers)

Idle gaps (no activity for >5 minutes) are excluded from screen time calculations.

### App Time Estimation

Per-app time is estimated using:
- Screenshot timestamps
- Window focus duration
- Active block boundaries

This gives a more accurate picture than raw capture count.

<Note>
Screen time excludes idle periods and screenshots from excluded apps (configured in Settings → Privacy).
</Note>

## Performance Notes

- Timeline mode loads up to 2000 screenshots per request
- Thumbnails are lazy-loaded as you scroll
- Hour groups are collapsed by default to improve render performance
- Activity data updates every 30 seconds via React Query

## Troubleshooting

<Warning>
**No captures showing?**
- Check that the daemon is running: `systemctl --user status rewindos-daemon`
- Verify capture is enabled in Settings → Capture
- Look for errors in `~/.rewindos/logs/daemon.log`
</Warning>

<Warning>
**Screen time seems incorrect?**
- Active time excludes idle gaps (normal behavior)
- Excluded apps don't count toward screen time
- Check Settings → Privacy for your excluded apps list
</Warning>

<Tip>
If you notice gaps in your timeline, the daemon may have been paused or the system was in sleep mode. Check daemon logs for context.
</Tip>

## Technical Details

History view uses these API endpoints:

- `browseScreenshots(start, end, app, limit)` — Fetch screenshots in time range
- `getActivity(start, end)` — Get app usage and activity stats
- `getActiveBlocks(start, end)` — Fetch active time blocks for screen time calculation
- `getTaskBreakdown(start, end)` — Per-app time estimates

See `src/features/history/hooks/useHistoryData.ts` for implementation details.
